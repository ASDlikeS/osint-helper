Open source intelligence (OSINT) is the collection and analysis of data gathered from open sources (overt sources and publicly available information) to produce actionable intelligence. OSINT is primarily used in national security, law enforcement, and business intelligence functions and is of value to analysts who use non-sensitive intelligence in answering classified, unclassified, or proprietary intelligence requirements across the previous intelligence disciplines.

Email Tools

haveIbeenPWNED?

IHaveBeenPwned is a website that allows users to check if their personal data has been compromised in a data breach. The site aggregates information from various known breaches and provides a searchable database where users can enter their email addresses to see if they've been affected. It helps raise awareness about online security and encourages users to change passwords or take other precautions if their data is found in a breach.

Email Rep.io

Description: One of the most well-known tools for checking if an email address has been compromised in a data breach. The service provides information on which breaches the address has appeared in. It also offers notifications when new breaches involving your email are discovered. Features: Supports searching by email addresses and domains, and provides breach notifications for future incidents.

Hunter.io

Description: Hunter.io is a tool for finding and verifying email addresses, especially useful for discovering corporate email addresses. It allows you to search for email addresses associated with specific domains and provides details about the reputation of these addresses. Features: Search by domains, find email addresses, verify the validity and reputation of email addresses, and offers an API.

Pipl.com

Description: Pipl is a powerful search engine for finding information about people, including email addresses, across various social platforms and public records. It helps track down user profiles and other details associated with a given email address. Features: Search by email, phone numbers, and physical addresses; aggregates information from multiple sources, including social networks.

HOLELE

Holele is an OSINT (Open Source Intelligence) tool designed for discovering and analyzing information related to online security and data breaches. It focuses on gathering detailed insights from publicly available data sources, helping users uncover connections between entities like email addresses, phone numbers, and other identifiers across the internet.

Central OPS

CentralOps.net is a network utility and information platform that provides users with several tools to analyze, troubleshoot, and gather information about internet connections and domain names. It’s a valuable resource for network administrators, security professionals, and anyone interested in understanding more about how internet services work.

The Harvester

TheHarvester is a simple to use, yet powerful tool designed to be used during the reconnaissance stage of a red team assessment or penetration test. It performs open source intelligence (OSINT) gathering to help determine a domain's external threat landscape. The tool gathers names, emails, IPs, subdomains, and URLs by using multiple public resources

That`s Them

That'sThem.com is an online platform designed to provide people-search services, helping users to look up individuals and gather publicly available information from various online databases. The platform is often used for finding people, verifying identities, or gathering details related to a specific person.

DeHashed

Dehashed.com is a specialized online service focused on providing tools for data breach search and account security. It allows users to search large databases of compromised or breached data, helping individuals and organizations identify whether their personal or organizational information has been exposed in data breaches.
