Open source intelligence (OSINT) is the collection and analysis of data gathered from open sources (overt sources and publicly available information) to produce actionable intelligence. OSINT is primarily used in national security, law enforcement, and business intelligence functions and is of value to analysts who use non-sensitive intelligence in answering classified, unclassified, or proprietary intelligence requirements across the previous intelligence disciplines.

Shodan

Shodan is a search engine, like Google, but instead of searching for websites, it searches for internet-connected devices — from routers and servers, to Internet of Things (IoT) devices, such as thermostats and baby monitors, to complex systems that govern a wide range of industries, including energy, power, and transportation. Shodan can find anything that connects directly to the internet — and if your internet-facing devices aren’t protected, Shodan can tell hackers everything they need to know to break into your network.

DorkSeacrh

Dorksearch is an OSINT (Open Source Intelligence) tool designed to search for specific information across search engines by utilizing advanced search queries known as "Google Dorks." Google Dorks are special search strings that allow users to uncover sensitive or hidden data on the internet, such as exposed databases, security vulnerabilities, or private documents. Dorksearch automates the process of crafting and executing these queries, helping investigators and security researchers find publicly available information that might otherwise be hard to discover.

CanaryTokens

The service allows you to find out the IP address of a person you are gathering information about. The key here is to convince them to click on a link, open an image, or document. The most harmless-looking method is using an image. You choose the "Web Image" method, upload an image to the CanaryTokens server, and provide your work email. After that, the service generates a unique token and link.

Inteligence.X

This multifunctional resource helps you look up information about an object using email, name, phone number, domain, IP, Bitcoin wallet, and more. The downside is that the set of features available in the free mode is quite limited. You can check if your data has been compromised, but to obtain detailed information, a paid subscription is required.
